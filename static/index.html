Essential guides for GST & Taxation.

❓ What is GST?

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is a unified indirect tax that replaced multiple taxes like VAT, Service Tax, and Excise. It is levied on the supply of goods and services.

Why it matters?

Proper compliance avoids penalties of up to 100% of the tax amount and ensures smooth inter-state trade.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about GST, compliance and TaxCalm.

Businesses with annual turnover exceeding ₹40 lakh (₹20 lakh for services) must register for GST. In North-Eastern and hilly states the threshold is ₹10 lakh.
Small businesses with turnover up to ₹1.5 crore can opt for the Composition Scheme, paying a fixed low rate (1%–5%) instead of regular GST. They cannot charge GST to customers and cannot claim ITC.
CGST and SGST are both charged on intra-state transactions — each gets 50% of the rate. IGST is charged on inter-state transactions and goes to the Centre before being shared with states.
ITC can be claimed when: (a) you hold a valid tax invoice, (b) the supplier has filed their GSTR-1, (c) goods/services have been received, and (d) tax has been paid. It is automatically reflected in your GSTR-2B.
GSTR-1 is a detailed statement of outward supplies (sales) filed by the 11th. GSTR-3B is a monthly self-assessment return summarising total sales, ITC and tax payable, filed by the 20th.
Data entered in TaxCalm is stored securely in your browser's local storage and on encrypted servers. We never share your data with third parties. All communication is over HTTPS.
Key schemes: MUDRA Loan (up to ₹10 lakh, no collateral), CGTMSE (credit guarantee up to ₹2 crore), SIDBI loans for machinery, and PM SVANidhi for street vendors. See the Schemes tab for details.
